Wilderness Peak 1-23-10

It was overcast in Seattle but as I drove south the rain started. I found the trailhead along 900 and found a spot in the small parking lot. I put on my rain pants and stuck my camera in the waterproof case before crossing a bridge to start the hike. I met a few hikers at the very beginning but didn't meet anyone else for about another mile. The trail was muddy but I expected some mud due to the rain. There were several bridges along the trail, a fun way to break up the hike. There were also a few trail junctions. I reached the first one about 0.5 miles into the hike. This junction started the loop, and following the guidance in my hiking book, I took the trail to the left to complete the hike in a clockwise direction. The trail was through the trees, but they were pretty green and there were several large boulders here and there. It wasn't the real dense forest I've hiked through on other trails, which I prefer. It's more mysterious and cave like in the dense forest, more of an adventure.

I came across this little boardwalk section which was really cool, one of my favorite parts of the hike. After taking a few pictures and trying to dry off the camera case, I encountered a couple so I tried stepping off of the main boardwalk onto the support logs below. It was a little tight but they managed to pass and I continued on the trail. About 1.5 miles into the hike I reached the Shy Bear Pass, a major trail junction. One of the trails led back to the main part of Cougar mountain but I continued on to Wilderness peak. As I hiked up, I noticed how hazy it was in the trees. I think I was hiking through a cloud.

Finally I reached the last trail junction for the short side trail up to the peak. At the top, instead of views like I am normally rewarded with, I found a bench - apparently dedicated in loving tribute to someone - and a journal to sign. The first page asked for hikers to leave a message for the shy bears, which is apparently a tradition on that trail. After leaving a short message I continued back to the trail and took the Wilderness Cliffs trail to complete the loop. There was a little side trail to an 'overlook', I got a glimpse of some mountains through the trees. It was still very cloudy near the top, but I did notice a bright light through the trees which I presumed was the hidden sun. I finally made it back to the loop junction and finished the last half mile to reach the car. It was not the most exciting hike but convenient and a good way to get some exercise.
