Los Osos Oaks 6/10/10

Cory was graduating from Cal Poly so I decided to head down to help him celebrate his accomplishments. I had business in California at the beginning of the week so I went up to SLO Wednesday after work, with a quick stop along the way in Danish Solvang for some smorgasbord. Thursday we decided to go for an adventure. Cory and Scott had found a park they wanted to check out, Los Osos Oaks. I just had a carry on so I had to leave my sunscreen at home. After we found a CVS so I could get my preferred sunscreen, we continued to the park. I was told it was more of a nature walk than a hike, so I had on some shorts and my running shoes. As soon as we started on the trail, we saw some awesome trees. I thought it was fun hiking through the desert like environment and I was admiring all of the different trees and flowers than what we have in Washington - or my normal stomping grounds in Washington. i.e. not the desert in eastern Washington. There were paths winding their way throughout the park so we got to choose our own adventure. We came upon one tree that apparently looked very inviting because before I knew it, both Scott and Cory had climbed up into it. I had my gorillapod along as usual, so I set it up for some pictures including a group one after I quickly scampered up the tree to join the guys.

After a little bit more walking we came upon this awesome grove of trees that screamed climbing playground to all of us. We each chose our own tree and proceeded to do some climbing. I know, sober tree climbing in the daylight - not the usual, but still fun. I guess that was my version of climbing for the weekend. After climbing the trees we continued along, passing more flowers and finding our way to a dead end along the fence of a nearby mobile home park. We turned around and decided that we should actually head back to the car since we were planning on going with a few people to Firestone for some beer tasting. We wanted to go back a different way, so after a few wrong turns we finally looped back to the parking lot. We even passed a couple critter friends hiding along the path. The walk was a lot of fun, due mainly to all of the great trees available for climbing.
