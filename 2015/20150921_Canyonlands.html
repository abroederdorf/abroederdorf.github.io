Canyonlands 9.21.15


We drove to Island in the Sky in Canyonlands National Park, arriving about 10am. Island in the Sky is a giant mesa, so it feels like you really are on an island in the sky. We did a bunch of short hikes in the park to experience lots of different parts of the park and views. Our first hike of the day was to Mesa Arch, a short, relatively flat hike just under a mile round trip. The Mesa Arch stands at the edge of the cliff, with great views of distant mountains and canyon rims through the arch. There were some large, very elaborate cairns marking the trail.


[Left] In front of the Mesa Arch. [Right] View through the Mesa Arch.

Our next hike was to the first and second overlook on Upheaval Dome, which over looked a large crater – cause of which is unknown. We started along a trail to reach the first overlook, about half a mile and 100’ of gain. Then we continued along rock slabs, following cairns, to what we thought was the second overlook. We stopped a little shy but got the basic effect of another view of the crater, and surrounding mesas.


View of Upheaval Dome


Hiking to the second overlook at Upheaval Dome

We drove just a little up the road to Whale Rock for our third hike, also about a mile roundtrip with 100’ of gain. This trail was almost entirely over rocky slabs following cairns. We reached the bump on the whale’s back, the highest point and where most people presumably stop, but Aaron wanted to run the entire length of the whale so we continued on the slab. It got a bit steeper but still within reason for running shoes.


[Left] On the trail to Whale Rock. [Right] Heading up past a giant cairn cemented to the rock.


[Left] Aaron running along Whale Rock. [Right] A view of Whale Rock from the road.

We then drove to White Rim Overlook and ate lunch on the rocks (all the picnic tables were taken) before starting out on our hike to the overlook just under a mile away. The trail was mostly over sand with some slabs, and very quiet. We met a total of 9 people while on the trail. The overlook is on the rim with great views of the white rim canyon below and distant La Sal Mountains. We took a break in the shade of a large rock formation before hiking back to the car.


[Left] Made it to the overlook. [Right] View of the white rim canyon

Our final hike of the day was to Grand View Point, another hike along the rim. This one is much more popular, but I enjoyed the White Rim hike more. You do follow the rim the entire time which is interesting, but the views at the end were more or less the same. To the west you do get to see a nearby mesa and to the northwest the Candlestick Tower.
