Arches 9.22.15

We woke up at 7 to get an early start into Arches National park, a recommendation since it gets pretty busy. We arrived at the entry to the park by 8am and drove to Wolfe Ranch first to do the 3 mile hike to Delicate Arch, a hike we figured would be the most popular so we should get it done early. We started along a trail, then hiked up a giant slab of rock, before winding our way through a bit more sand and along more rocks. There were several people at the arch when we arrived, but it certainly appears to be the quintessential arch. Aaron ran across the slab, much to the amusement of other hikers, to get a photo beneath the arch.


[Left] Heading up the trail to Delicate Arch, heading up the large slab on the left. [Right] Delicate Arch.

Next on our list was the Devils Garden hike, on which we saw 6 more arches. We started with the easy arches, taking the spur trails to Tunnel Arch and Pine Tree Arch. We saw Landscape Arch form the trail to Double O Arch, then took the spur trails to see Partition Arch and Navajo Arch. Partition Arch had some amazing views through it.


[Left] Tunnel Arch. [Right] Pine Tree Arch


[Left] Landscape Arch. [Right] Heading up some rock fins on the way to Double O arch.


[Left & Middle] Partition Arch. [Right] Navajo Arch

Finally we finished the almost mile hike to see Double O Arch, hiking atop some rock fins. Double O Arch is two arches stacked on top of each other. Weather had looked threatening all day so rather than continue on to see the Dark Angel Tower, we headed back to get passed the rock slabs before it started raining (turns out it never did rain).


[Left] Hiking to Double O Arch. [Right] Double O Arch, two arches stacked on top of each other

Our last stop of the day was at the Windows loop. We first saw Turret Arch, the continued on to the south window and north window. These windows were very large and reminded me more of arches rather than the windows I saw at Bryce Canyon.


[Left] Turret Arch. [Right] Window Arches.

On Wednesday we woke up at 6am for our hot air balloon ride at 7am. Luckily they shuttled us from our campground so that was convenient. We signed some waivers and loaded into the 15 passenger van to head to the launch site. There they gave us a brief overview and then started inflating the balloon with cool air via fans. Once it was inflated they warmed the air to get the balloon to stand upright. We loaded into the four compartments of the basket, with three or four adults per compartment, and lifted off.


Getting the balloon ready

We shared a compartment with a very nice couple from Maryland. The balloon ride was very gentle and we got some great views of the surrounding mesas, mountains, and hillsides. We floated north-northeast into Arches National Park in the Salt Valley. Along the way we came down so low Aaron and I thought we might land, but then we lifted off again. We were close enough to watch all of the rabbits scurrying from bush to bush to take cover from our balloon.


Flying over Arches National Park

We landed half on the road running through Salt Valley, and half in the brush along the road. The ground crew arrived about 10 minutes after landing, so we got to watch the pilot get help in half deflating the balloon. Aaron helped hold the balloon so it wouldn’t blow into nearby road signs. Since we weren’t supposed to land in the national park, they quickly shuttled us back to the campground while the pilot waited to work with the rangers. Back at the campground we enjoyed a champagne toast with everyone before hitting the road home.
