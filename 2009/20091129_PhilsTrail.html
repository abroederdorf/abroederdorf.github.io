Phils Trail - Thrush Gap Loop 11-29-09

I had hoped to get a couple of hikes in during the long Thanksgiving break weekend, but I was enjoying relaxing on the couch either watching movies or napping way too much. Although I woke up late yet again on Sunday, it was early enough to get a shorter hike in. I made my stop at Safeway for gas and started off the day on a bad foot when the gas pump didn't automatically shut off and gas went spilling onto the pavement - grrr, wasted gas. The hike I decided to do was suppose to be 5 miles long on Squak Mountain. The trailhead is actually only a few miles from where I lived in Renton a few summers ago.

There were a few cars at the trailhead and a couple of people getting horses ready for a ride. Unfortunately I was not quick enough and the horses set off before me. Soon I caught up, but the horses were large, smelly animals that make it hard to pass so I had to fall inline behind them. I soon found out that I hate hiking near horses. I took unnecessary breaks to try and distance myself but I usually caught up. Another pet peeve is that fido's owners are expected to pick up after fido along the trail, but horse owners aren't expected to do the same. And a horse's pile is much larger than Fido's pile. Finally, we came upon a tree across the trail. The top of the tree was at or just above my waist and was apparently high enough to stop the horses. They turned back and I gladly continued horse free.

There were several creeks running on the mountainside and they were surprisingly loud. When I would finally get a glimpse of the noisy water, it was always a lot smaller than I expected. There were a lot of signs along the trail, probably because there were a lot of junctions. I just followed the signs on the way to Central Peak. I had my hiking guide book with me but the mileage differed from that posted on the signs, so I was getting confused about where I was, and thus, which forks to take. After passing through a hiker's gate, I soon entered a different world. The trail became more narrow and more green. After a little way, I thought I had taken the wrong trail. I decided to continue another quarter mile just in case, and before the end of that I found another trail junction and sign, assuring me I was headed in the right direction on the right path.

I entered a more dense forest that seemed eerie. It seemed darker, possibly having to due with the fog I encountered, and there were many more fallen trees than throughout the rest of the hike. I kept looking for the Bullitt Lodge remnants mentioned in my book but thought I had missed the fireplace. I finally made it to the Bullitt Fireplace trail mentioned in my book and soon found myself at a giant fireplace, it was really quite grand.

Less than a half mile from the fireplace I finally made it to the top of Central Peak, and got to see all of the radio towers up there. I back tracked 0.1 miles to the junction with Old Griz trail, to continue back down the mountain to my car. After 0.6 miles on that, I came to another junction with the Phils Trail and continued on that. I had to hike for just over a quarter mile along the service access road, but luckily I jumped back on the trail, soon completing the loop and backtracking down the trail the rest of the way.

Near the trailhead, by where the trail crosses the service access road, is another little trail - the Pretzel Loop interpretive trail. I decided to go check out what the board said and much to my surprise, found a delightful story about a field mouse, great for engaging children. After completing that loop I finally made it back to the trailhead and took off for home. Only 3.5 miles left until I hit my 200 mile mark.
