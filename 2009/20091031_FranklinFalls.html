Franklin Falls 10-31-09

I was ready for another weekend of hiking. I had been keeping a close eye on the weather reports, like I usually do, hoping for some nice weather for the weekend. I really like the weather forecast from NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration). You can select a specific area on the Google map and get the 7 day forecast. It’s nice because most of my hikes are not very close to a town that I can get a forecast for. Saturday was calling for rain and Sunday was supposed to be partly cloudy. I was kind of excited to try out my new rain gear, my Helly Hansen rain pants and my REI Traverse 30 backpack for women. I had technically used my new pack the previous weekend for my hikes in the North Cascades, but they were on a beautiful day so I didn’t have a reason to try the built in pack cover. To complete my rain gear I also had my new-ish shell (rain jacket), the Stoic 2.0 Shell for women and my waterproof camera case, which I always think makes my camera look like a toy. The case is big and bulky but at least I get to keep taking pictures. I’ve gotten a lot of ‘Oh, that’s a good idea’ comments from hikers that I pass on the trail, too.

For Saturday hiking I decided to go towards Snoqualmie Pass for a couple of hikes. I would start with Franklin Falls and then drive a quarter mile to the trailhead for Melakwa Lake after. Sunday I had been planning on hiking to Wallace Falls off of Highway 2 but since my boots were soaked, I still had blisters, and I like sleeping, I made the decision to waste a gorgeous day and stay home until football later that afternoon (which by the way, we won our playoff game with a comeback drive in the last 12 seconds of the game and I threw the game winning touchdown pass, although really it was my teammate who managed to come down with the catch). Back to Saturday though, I think one of the two trail sites I usually check (Washington Trails Association and Pacific Northwest Hikes) had a special article on waterfall hikes, and since we all know how much I like waterfalls, I added most of the hikes to my to-do list of hikes. Franklin Falls was one of those hikes and since it was close to Melakwa Lake, which I had heard of, I decided to combine the two. Franklin Falls is only a 2 mile hike while Melakwa Lake is 9 miles round trip.

I haven’t been doing as well lately with leaving early in the morning as I always intend. I think I got in my car about 7:45 am and it took just over an hour to arrive at the trailhead. I took exit 47 on I-90 and drove another 3 miles or so east on forest roads to the trailhead for Franklin Falls. The trails are near the Denny Creek campground. It’s like a little oasis between east and westbound I-90, I couldn’t believe it. And luckily the hike to Franklin Falls, which follows I-90 closely, had the South Fork Snoqualmie River that drowned out any car sounds.

It wasn’t actually raining yet but everything was pretty wet and the trees were dripping on me. I had assumed it would be raining all day so I already had my rain pants and jacket on. The trail was very nice despite the wetness and water. There were some pretty large puddles and it was a little muddy, but I can imagine during the dry summer months that this trail is a treat to hike, especially with Franklin Falls as the final destination. The river was flowing very fast, I was actually quite surprised. One section of the trail had been overtaken by the river and was completely submerged so I reluctantly hiked off trail around the 5 – 10 foot section (I try to always avoid ‘meadow stomping’). It seemed like there was water flowing from every which way into the already raging river. There were quite a few nice little waterfalls along the river. The elevation gain was very moderate; I think maybe 400’ in total. Before I knew it I was descending the last 50 feet of the rocky trail to the pool at the base of the waterfall. Franklin Falls was quite a sight! It was much better than I anticipated. It also surprised me to see I-90 right next to the waterfall, just above it. For all the times I have driven on I-90, I never knew that Franklin Falls existed. After a few pictures of the falls I headed back down the trail to my car, to continue on for my second hike of the day. The hike to Franklin Falls was really nice and a great way to warm up for my day of hiking.

I learned last weekend that MotionBased would only upload my current track log so when I created plots of distance, elevation, time, and speed it included both hikes. I used my awesome MS Paint skills to split the plots and post them with their respective hike. Bring on the nerd data!
