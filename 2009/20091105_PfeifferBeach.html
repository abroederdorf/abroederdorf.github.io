Pfeiffer Beach 11-5-09

The next adventure stop was not so much of a hike, but I still grabbed the GPS. After some cryptic directions – apparently the locals like to keep the beach hidden so there is no clear signage on the road – and 2 miles of a narrow, curvy road, we arrived at Pfeiffer Beach. After a very short walk through some trees you are on the beach and what an amazing sight! Waves crashing against giant rock formations, blue green water, white surf, and sea life! After taking a gazillion pictures trying to capture the waves, we continued down the beach, stopping for some climbing up an inviting looking large rock. Continuing a little further down the beach we found a seal napping on the beach near the cliffs. We turned back and found a good hill of sand to rest on and enjoy the ocean from. A nosy seagull was staking us out, hoping we had handouts but he was out of luck. He made some awesome footprints in the sand though. On the way back to the car we explored this cavernous looking area and found some mini tide pools with star fish, anemones, and crashing waves.
