Hiking Stats

As clearly seen, I didn't do a great job of keeping up with activities this year. Fortunately I still got out for quite a few hikes. When I'd have time hiking did seem to be a lower barrier of lost skills so we'd head out. Finally got up to Oyster Dome in the early spring. Towards the end of the spring quarter we got out for a couple of more adventurous hikes - one up Mount Margaret which mostly follows the Twin Lakes trail. The other was a snow free ascent up Guye Peak, the first for both Aaron and I. We missed the trail to Guye Peak after the log crossing and ended up hiking up through the basin hoping to find it, finally turning around when we started gaining elevation towards Red Mountain. Turns out after you cross the log you take a left and that trail leads up Guye Peak - who would have guessed a left?

Aaron's college roommate Dan came out in August after the summer quarter had ended. We went up to the North Cascades and did some fun hikes I hadn't done in several years - Blue Lake on the really hot day, and Cascade Pass to a view over Lake Doubtful. It was nice to get out in the North Cascades, my first time for the year. I mostly stuck to I-90 hikes for ease of access and the lower time commitment with my busy schedule.
