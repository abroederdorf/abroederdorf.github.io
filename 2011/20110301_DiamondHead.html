Diamond Head 3.1.11

Approach

  • Fly aboard a Boeing 757: 2675 miles, 6 hours 8 minutes
  • Travel around Oahu via bus, rental car, and by foot sight seeing, swimming, snorkeling, scuba diving, and eating: 230 miles, 4 days
  • Ride the #23 Bus from hotel: 2.1 miles, 15 minutes

Gear

  • Clothing: Running shoes, shorts, and t-shirt
  • Gear: Camera and free hotel tote bag

Essentials

  • Navigation: Park map brochure
  • Sun Protection: Sunglasses and SPF 50 sunscreen
  • Light Source: Head Lamps
  • Water
  • Food: Summit treats (gummy worms and corn bread)
  • Extra Clothing: Bandana

Tips

  • Travel light, the bus is crowded
  • Arrive early, trail gets crowded quickly with tourists
  • Beware of the roaming roosters
  • Complete the hike fast enough to use bus transfer to return to hotel

Report

We caught the number 23 bus from right outside our hotel. I finished applying my sun protection and ate some honey nut cheerios as we waited. We squeezed into the back of the bus and found a couple seats for the 15 minute ride to the Diamond Head state park. Once we departed the bus, we joined in the line of tourists headed up the road to the park entrance. After paying a dollar each we grabbed a park map brochure and continued to the top, paying attention to the warning signs stating the hike covered 560’ in elevation gain over 1.5 miles round trip – very strenuous!

The beginning of the trail was well maintained, no uneven surfaces. We soon found ourselves on a dirt path for the rest of the hike. Making sure to watch out for rocks, we quickly ascended through the crowds with the view of the crater below us. We finally reached the stairs – 74 up to the 225 foot long tunnel. The tunnel has had lights installed and we did not use our headlamps. Once we emerged from the tunnel, we had another 99 steps up to reach the fire control station. From there we went up 52 steps to the observation deck, exiting to the outside of the crater with 54 steps separating us from our summit – and most importantly, summit treats.

Roughly 25 minutes after entering the park we had arrived at our summit, 560’ above the crater floor where we had started. We took a few quick pictures on the summit and descended down to the observation area for our treats. The summit was very buggy and crowded so there was no sense in staying up there. After our fill of gummy worms and taking in the views, we headed back down, passing most everyone on our way. Our next object – catch the bus to Leonard’s Bakery to get some tasty cinnamon sugar malasadas – mmmm, dounts!
